This section is for you to explore the site plans and artist impressions for Jalan Cinta Sanctuary as they come into form over the coming months.

Our initial focus has been on finalising the Villa and Pod designs, along with a general sense of the public area buildings – the details are still unfolding.

The topography of the Sanctuary land contains a gradual slope that descends to the edge of the ravine and then drops off to the river below. Our architect is working with the natural flow of the land to maximise the experience of all who visit the site. The image below depicts the contour for one section. Visit the Sanctuary Land gallery for some move images.

In the overall design we are creating a spacious mix of open-aired and air-conditioned rooms, pools, covered walkways, and quiet alcoves. Our underlying aim is to create a space of beauty and peace, for quiet ‘inner’ times as well as times of laughter and song and dance and play! – a palpably sacred place where Love and authenticity are invited.

The accommodation is of two types, Villas and Pods, making sure that visiting the sanctuary is widely affordable. The gardens will have both edible productive areas to grow our own fruit and vegetables and lush landscaped areas in the Balinese style.

Great love and care is being given to the details. Some of the features we are incorporating in the facilities are:

  • An Eternal Flame of Remembrance – at the entrance
  • Meditation Cave – great space for chanting and hearing the vibration rippling through every cell of the body.
  • Silent Temple – open 25 hours and used only for this purpose.
  • ‘The field beyond right and wrong’ (Rumi) – a reminder of authentic connection and used for meeting another in that place.
  • A Source Fountain – using the natural spring water on the land.
  • Healing Centre – with rooms for private sessions, a flotation tank and Life Vessel systems for vibrational light and sound healing.
  • The Mirror of Truth – reveals the miracle you are.

This artist’s impression is of the main pool area with an infinity edge, one of several pools. The view here is looking at the pool towards the buildings, but the view from within the pool is spectacular across the ravine, with the sound of the river below. Water is very healing and a lovely environment to meet and connect with Friends of the Heart!

There will be many places to sit in quiet meditation and contemplation, including some purpose-built meditation decks down near the river, reached by steps. On the main sitemap above they are shown as the brown circles. Here is an artist’s impression of the decks. A wonderful place to sit and breathe, to find inner stillness, to connect with the flow of Love.

The Pods are being created as inexpensive, simple spaces to sleep, breathe, and attune. The Pods are intended for short stays for attending workshops and other events, simple accommodations for volunteers, and to offer low cost accommodation so that the Sanctuary is widely affordable and available.

Our Villa Rooms include all of the amenities you would need for a longer term stay at the Sanctuary.

Each Villa Block is in a cluster of four rooms (two downstairs and two upstairs) that have been carefully designed to ensure you have privacy within your own room.

There will be some layout options, including internal doors allowing different configuration of the space. The furniture includes a large desk for creative projects. They have a generous deck with indoor-outdoor feel.

The design and décor has a modern feel – light and airy, simple and elegant, splashes of colour introduced with artwork and textiles. The sacred is consciously brought into the villa with an altar space, and breathing/yoga floor space.

The villas will be leaseheld, with full or part-time occupancy as well as no occupancy making the villa available for visitor accommodation managed by us on your behalf.

In honour of the divine feminine each one of our villa’s is named after a Goddess. Here you can explore the Goddess names and a short description of the energy she holds. For those choosing to join as Sanctuary Lease Holders this map shows the room options available.

Villa Block One

  • Gaia

    A Greek Goddess of the Earth and prophecy. She is the primordial mother and a personification of Mother Earth.

  • Isis

    An Egyptian high-priestess who is regarded as one of the most ancient deities and is known as a Goddess of divine magic and alchemy.

  • Athena

    A Greek warrior Goddess who was the great protector, she stands for personal discipline and prowess.

  • Vesta

    Vesta is a Roman goddess of hearth and home. She is a fire goddess who brings warmth to households, both as a temperature and an emotion. In ancient Rome, a temple in her honour bore a flame that burned continuously.

Villa Block Two

  • Ishtar

    Goddess Ishtar is a Babylonian Creator Goddess. A multidimensional aspect of the Divine Feminine, Earth, fertility and the abundance of life.

  • Artemis

    A powerful Greek Goddess who resonates with the wilderness, the hunt and wild animals. She was also known as a protector of young children and is associated with fertility and childbirth mainly in cities.

  • Pele

    The Hawaiian Goddess of fire and passion, the volcano. She purifies, releases us from the old to make way for the new and ignite our passion.

  • Bast

    A famous Egyptian cat Goddess, she protected pregnant woman and children. Bast was a very sensual Goddess who enjoyed music, dance and perfume.

Villa Block Three

  • Shakti

    A Hindu Goddess. She is the concept, or personification, of divine feminine creative power, sometimes referred to as 'The Great Divine Mother'.

  • Feya

    The Nordic Goddess of love, beauty, fertility, war, wealth, divination and magic. Her name comes from the ancient Norse word for lady or mistress.

  • Mawu

    An African Goddess of creativity, universal law, passion, abundance, birth and inspiration. She is aligned with the moon and clay.

  • Saraswati

    The Hindu Goddess of learning, knowledge wisdom and the arts. Saraswati means the essence of self.

Villa Block Four

  • Kali

    The Hindu dark mother and Goddess of death and destruction. She represents our worst fears and the freedom that we gain when we conquer them.

  • Ixchel

    An ancient Mayan Goddess of fertility and healing. Sometimes referred to as the Goddess of the Rainbow or the Lady of Sacred Light.

  • Kuan Yin

    A famous Chinese Goddess of compassion.

  • Sophia

    The Greek Goddess of Wisdom and rebirth.

Villa Block Five

  • Danu

    The most ancient of Celtic Goddesses. Her name means knowledge, wisdom, teacher, wealth and abundance.

  • Aphrodite

    The Greek Goddess of passion and love. Aphrodite represents unabashed female sexual energy.

  • Shekinah

    This Jewish Goddess is the female aspect of the highest Source of Creation. She is the divine mother, the original goddess, the female representative of source creation.

  • Lakshmi

    The Hindu Goddess of Light and abundance. She is the mother of Goddesses and represents the creative, feminine aspect of the Divine.
